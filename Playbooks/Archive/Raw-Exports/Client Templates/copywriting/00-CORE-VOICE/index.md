## Voice Consistency Framework

### Universal Principles (All Contexts)
1. **Simplicity:** Fifth-grade reading level, complex ideas made accessible
2. **Conversational Flow:** Sounds like organized speaking
3. **Natural Personality:** Authentic, never forced
4. **Scannable Structure:** Easy to read quickly
5. **Big Picture Connections:** Relate details to larger purpose

### Context-Specific Applications

#### Quick Communications (Slack, brief emails)
- Ultra-simple language
- Minimal personality elements
- Clear action focus
- Skip deeper voice elements

#### Professional Content (client communication, proposals)
- Simplicity + clarity
- Subtle dry humor
- Short, relevant big-picture notes (not long abstract tangents)
- Collaborative tone
- Clear next steps when appropriate (not everything needs to be prescriptive)
- Direct by default, nuanced when needed (single honest hedges ok for complex/contrarian ideas)

#### Personal/Casual Content (blogs, personal notes)
- Full personality range
- All humor elements
- Philosophical undertones
- Personal references
- Natural flow

#### Technical Writing (documentation, SOPs)
- Maintain conversational tone
- Use analogies for complex concepts
- Keep structured organization
- Preserve helpful personality

### Voice Validation Checklist

Before publishing any content:
- ‚úì Does this sound like me explaining to a colleague over coffee?
- ‚úì Would a fifth-grader understand the main concepts?
- ‚úì Is any humor subtle enough it might be missed?
- ‚úì Have I connected this to the bigger picture?
- ‚úì Does my personality come through naturally?
- ‚úì Is the structure scannable and organized?
