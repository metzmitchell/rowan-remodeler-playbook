## Usage Guidelines

### When to Include Personal References
- **Medium content (blog posts, articles)** - Light personal anecdotes when relevant
- **Long-form content (scripts, detailed posts)** - More personal references and stories
- **Personal writing** - Full range of personal details and context
- **Storytelling pieces** - Natural integration of life details

### When NOT to Include
- **Professional client communication** - Keep minimal and only when strategically relevant
- **Quick communications** - Skip personal touches for efficiency
- **Technical content** - Unless personal experience directly relates to topic

### Natural Integration Examples
- **Location reference:** "Like a good [Local Geographic Feature], the best strategy isn't always obvious"
- **Hobby background:** "Building a brand is like [Hobby/Skill] - simple fundamentals, but the real art comes from practice"
- **Interest area:** Subtle depth in thinking without explicit references
- **Lifestyle:** "Clear strategy, like a [Personal Lifestyle Moment], reveals what's really important"

## Voice Consistency Notes
- **Never force personal references** - they should feel natural and relevant
- **Maintain subtle integration** - personal touches enhance rather than dominate
- **Keep authentic** - use real experiences, not manufactured ones
- **Update regularly** - add new information and experiences as learned
