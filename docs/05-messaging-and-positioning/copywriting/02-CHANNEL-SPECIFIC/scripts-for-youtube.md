# YouTube Script Preferences

## Natural Speaking Patterns
- **Write for speech, not reading** - Scripts should sound like casual conversation
- **Include natural filler words** - "So", "Look", "Yeah" when they feel natural
- **Add conversational transitions** - "Let me show you what I mean"

### Cohesion and Connective Language
- **Use function words and discourse markers for flow** - Favor connective tissue like coordinating/subordinating conjunctions (and, but, so, because, while), determiners (the, this, that), prepositions (for, with, about), and discourse markers (then, so, look) to bind ideas into smooth, continuous sentences.
- **Prefer connected sentences over fragments** - Lean into compound/complex sentences that read naturally on a teleprompter; avoid telegraphic, list-like stacks of short sentences.
- **Contractions are fine; avoid clipped words** - Keep "it's" and "you're," but do not use shortened words or slangy abbreviations (info, vid, pics, btw), and write "and" instead of ampersands.

## Avoid Kitchy Language
- **No overused phrases** - Avoid "Boom!", "Mind blown?", "Game-changer!", "Here's the deal"
- **No question-answer patterns** - Don't use "Solution? Solution." or "Problem? Answer." - just state the answer directly
- **Remove inflammatory language** - Avoid "drives me crazy" or overly emotional statements
- **Sound observational, not excited** - Dry humor over enthusiasm
- **Natural expressions** - "That's pretty cool" vs "That's absolutely incredible!"
- **Avoid forced inclusivity** - Don't overuse "our industry" when unnatural

## Script Formatting (Movie Script Style)
- **Use brackets for all non-spoken elements** - [SCREEN: Show website], [CUT TO: Face cam]
- **Keep stage directions concise** - 5-10 words max in brackets
- **Action callouts** - [TYPE ON SCREEN: "exact text"], [UNSCRIPTED: brief description]

## TELEPROMPTER FORMAT REQUIREMENTS
- **Minimize bracketed stage directions** - Keep technical notes to absolute minimum in main script
- **Write clean, flowing text** - Optimize for reading on teleprompter without visual clutter
- **Use natural paragraph breaks** - Create reading rhythm that works with teleprompter scrolling
- **Avoid excessive formatting** - Bold, italics, and special characters can clutter teleprompter display
- **Include source citations naturally** - Weave citations into spoken content flow rather than bracketed notes
- **Create companion production documents** - Separate detailed screenshare outlines and technical notes
- **Focus on spoken-word flow** - Prioritize readability over production guidance in main script
- **Natural pausing points** - Structure paragraphs to allow for natural breathing and emphasis

### Teleprompter Script Structure:
```
Main Script (Teleprompter-Optimized):
- Clean spoken content only
- Natural paragraph breaks
- Minimal technical interruptions
- Sources woven into dialogue

Companion Production Outline:
- Detailed screenshare instructions
- Technical setup notes
- B-roll requirements
- Editing notes
```

## Content Structure Preferences
- **Start with end result** - Show the final product before explaining how it works
- **Focus on practical value** - What can viewers actually do with this information
- **Include live demonstrations** - Show real work happening, not just talking about it
- **Remove formal section headers** - Let content flow naturally without "The Problem", "The Solution" labels
- **Use connectors for flow** - Add "and", "but", "or" to connect ideas instead of choppy sentences
- **Make content actionable** - Specific steps and examples over abstract concepts
- **End with gratitude** - "Thanks for watching" instead of challenge CTAs

## Voice Characteristics to Maintain
- **Conversational but organized** - Like talking to a friend who knows their stuff
- **Confident but not arrogant** - Share knowledge without talking down
- **Personal but professional** - Mention real details when relevant

## SOURCE CITATION REQUIREMENTS
- **Always include sources when presenting data, statistics, or research findings**
- **For market research or data-heavy scripts**: Use comprehensive guidelines from `/90-GENERIC-PROMPTS/Market-Research-Script-Template.md`
- **For other scripts**: Basic citation format:
  - Cite sources naturally: "according to [Source] report" or "based on [Study]"
  - Include sources section at end
  - Mention key sources during presentation for credibility
  - Use primary sources, not secondary research documents

Notes:
- Keep energy high but natural
- Emphasize accessibility and real results
- Always verify and cite sources for credibility
- Optimize scripts for teleprompter reading while maintaining all production guidance in companion documents

Important! Use a casual voice, natural talking flow - writing a script to be spoken.

#### YouTube Markdown Formatting:
YouTube allows some Markdown-style formatting in video descriptions, but with specific syntax:

- **Bold:** Wrap your text in single asterisks, like *this*, to make it bold. For example, typing *Important update* in your YouTube video description will display as **Important update**.

- **Italics:** Wrap your text in single underscores, like _this_, for italics.

- **Strikethrough:** Wrap your text in hyphens, like -this-, for strikethrough.

**Important formatting notes:**
- Use single asterisks for bold (not double asterisks like standard Markdown)
- Use single underscores for italics (not double underscores)
- Ensure there are spaces around the formatting symbols to render formatting correctly
- Bold text will work, while words directly next to punctuation may not format as expected
- Example: *bold text* will work, while *bold text* may not format properly
